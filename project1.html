About us


Everything is about couscous




Tunisian couscous is one of countless variations of this delicious, versatile North African dish consisting of semolina served with a vegetable and meat-based broth.

In Morocco, couscous is traditionally prepared and eaten on Friday, the day of the great weekly prayer for Muslims. A traditional Moroccan couscous, simple to make.




Durum wheat in semolina can be replaced by barley in a dish which is then called abelbul, aberbus and barbusa in Morocco and Algeria and meltut in Tunis.


This couscous recipe with spinach from Niger was made to accompany you on a trip to Africa
